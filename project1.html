SFU Pet Adoption

Project 04: SFU Pet Adoption was the final project for the IAT 235 course, completed in a team of four. The project focused on designing and developing a functional, user-centered website. My primary responsibilities included ensuring visual consistency with our wireframes and building the main navigation pages using HTML and CSS.
Throughout the project, we used GitHub to collaboratively manage and track our progress while balancing both individual and group contributions.

The project progressed through several key stages. We began with individual webpage design ideation, where each team member explored initial concepts. These ideas were then iterated and refined by integrating UI/UX principles learned in IAT 201. As a group, we established the overall structure of the website and aligned on a cohesive design direction. Finally, each member was responsible for developing a specific webpage, contributing to the complete site.

One of the main challenges we encountered was differing perspectives on user interaction and user experience design. Each team member approached design decisions based on their own understanding, which sometimes led to inconsistencies. To address this, we engaged in open discussions, supported our ideas with research, and considered diverse user needs to reach informed, collective decisions.

Through this project, I developed stronger collaboration and communication skills, particularly in working with team members who had different viewpoints. I also learned the importance of supporting design decisions with evidence and maintaining consistency across a shared product.

To present this project to different audiences, I would tailor the level of detail and focus accordingly. For general audiences, I would highlight the overall goals, process, and final outcome. For more technical or design-focused audiences, I would emphasize specific challenges, design decisions, and the application of UI/UX principles, supported by visual examples from each stage of development.
