Canvas App Redesign was a case study completed for IAT 201 in a team, which focused on understanding and improving User Experience and User Interaction through redesign. The goal of the project was to analyze an existing application and adapt it for a specific user group with accessibility needs. Our team chose to focus on users with colour vision deficiency, who reportedly experience difficulties when navigating the university’s learning platform, Canvas.

The original Canvas interface relies heavily on colour-based indicators to communicate information and guide navigation. While this system is effective for many users, it can create confusion and frustration for those with colourblindness, as important distinctions between elements become unclear or indistinguishable. This revealed a gap in accessibility that directly impacts usability.

To address this issue, our team redesigned key interface elements by carefully adjusting colour saturation and contrast to improve visibility. We introduced alternative visual cues such as shapes, icons, and patterns to ensure that information could be understood without relying solely on colour. These changes aimed to create a more inclusive and intuitive user experience while maintaining the overall functionality of the app.

Through this project, I gained a deeper understanding of accessibility-focused design and the importance of considering diverse user needs early in the design process. It also strengthened my ability to evaluate existing interfaces, identify usability issues, and propose thoughtful, user-centered solutions.
