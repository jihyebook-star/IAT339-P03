Study Permit Extension Webpage

The Canadian Government Study Permit Extension Webpage Redesign was a case study completed as part of IAT 201 in a team setting. The objective of the project was to critically analyze an existing government website, identify key usability flaws, and redesign the experience to better reflect the needs, contexts, and constraints of real users.

Our focus was on the current Canadian Government Study Permit Extension webpage, which we found does not sufficiently prioritize user experience. Key issues included a lack of intuitive navigation, limited visual clarity, and insufficient language accessibility for a diverse user base. These shortcomings create unnecessary cognitive load, particularly for international students who may already be navigating complex and stressful processes.

In addition, our team identified elements of dark patterns, which are a design that can unintentionally or intentionally guide users toward confusion or a specific direction. The structure and presentation of information made the process feel overly complicated, requiring users to spend additional time and effort to complete essential tasks. This design approach can lead to frustration, errors, or even abandonment of the process, potentially resulting in delays.

Through this project, we aimed to reconstruct the webpage with a stronger emphasis on clarity, accessibility, and user-centered design principles, ultimately creating a more transparent and supportive experience.
